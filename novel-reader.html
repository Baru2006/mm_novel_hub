Chapter 1: The Crimson Sunset

The dust of the plains settled as the sun bled across the horizon, painting the thousand pagodas of Bagan in hues of crimson and gold. General Kyaw Htin stood on the battlements of the city wall, his gaze fixed on the endless expanse where the enemy hordes were gathering like a storm cloud. The air was thick with tension, a silent prelude to the symphony of war that was to come.

He tightened his grip on the hilt of his dha, the cold steel a familiar comfort against his calloused palm. For generations, his family had guarded this sacred city, a jewel of the empire. Now, it fell to him to defend it against the greatest threat it had ever faced. The whispers of the court spoke of betrayal, of a kingdom weakened from within. But here, on the walls, there was only duty.

"They are preparing for the first wave," a young captain said, his voice barely a whisper. Kyaw Htin nodded, not taking his eyes off the enemy camp. The horns would sound soon. The drums of war would beat. And Bagan would answer.
